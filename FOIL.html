FOIL Method

The FOIL Method is commonly used to help combine equations within parenthesis and that are being multiplyed. These are also used a lot within quadratics

The FOIL Method


How does the FOIL Method Work?

F: First/The first value of the equation or the front of the first parenthesis

O: Outer/The last value within the first parentesis

I: Inner/The first value within the second parenthesis

L: Last/The last value of the second parenthesis


How to Solve Equations with FOIL Method

The first thing you do is multiply the 2x (first) with 5x (the other first)

You then multiply the outer numbers (the 2x and -8)

You next multiply the inner numbers (the 3 and 5x)

You lastly muliply the last number of the equations (the 3 and -8)

Once you multiply each of the parts, you should get F: 10x^2 O: -16x I: 15x L: -24

You then put the products into one equation, 10x^2 - 16x + 15x - 24

To complete this problem, you will need to combine like terms to create a quadratic formula.

the like terms in these equation are the -16x and the 15x as they both contain x to the first power. Combining these like terms gives you -x

You lastly add the remainder to the equation, giving you the answer 10x^2 - x - 24
