Quadratic Functions

Quadratic Functions include the common and well known equation "y = ax^2 + bx + c". This lesson also include its use in graphs.

Quadratic Functions


How to Write a Linear Function

y = Your answer and placement on the y-axis

a = How steep your slope is and whehter you equation goes up or down

b = How far from the vertical line your equation is

c = The y-intercept

x = the point you are trying to find on the x-axis

result: y = ax^2 + bx + c


How to Solve a Quadratic Function

The following equation: y= -x^2 - 2x + 3

The y-intercept (3) can be seen on the y line at point 3

The slope (-_x) can also be written as -1x

To begin making the plot points for your graph, you need to input the various numbers from the x-axis around the y-intercept

When you solve the equation inputing the numbers, you will begin to see the line of the equation geginning to take form

A trend that you will see with quadratic functions is that each side of the points are the same, you can sometimes find one side of the vertex, the top or bottom of the line, a fill out the corresponding side
